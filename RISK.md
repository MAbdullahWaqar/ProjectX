# Risk Note — RealDoor

RealDoor is **assistive, not adjudicative**. This note lists the risks a tool in this space
carries, and the concrete control that mitigates each — all demonstrable in the app.

| # | Risk | Why it matters | Control in RealDoor | Residual risk |
|---|------|----------------|---------------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Acting as gatekeeper** (approving/denying/scoring/ranking) | The minimum bar: a tool that decides eligibility replaces the human it should support and can unlawfully screen people out. | `compareIncome` cannot return a verdict (no `eligible` field, asserted by test); every comparison ships a human-decision deflection; decision questions are refused. | A renter may still *infer* a conclusion from the numbers — mitigated by always-visible "this is information, not a decision" framing. |
| 2 | **Hidden proxies / disparate impact** | Inferring protected traits or using demographic/behavioral/landlord-revenue features encodes discrimination. | Strict **field allowlist** (published in-app with each field's purpose) + a **denylist** of sensitive fields that are never extracted or inferred. No ranking or scoring anywhere. | Free-text notes are renter-authored; RealDoor never parses them for features. |
| 3 | **Prompt injection via documents** | A malicious/embedded instruction could try to force "eligible" or fake confidence. | Document text is **never** sent to an instruction-following model; injection lines are detected, shown to the renter, and ignored. Injection test proves real fields still extract and confidence stays set by the YTD cross-check, not forced to 100%. | Novel injection phrasings may evade the *detector* (informational only) but **cannot** alter behavior, since there is no instruction-executing path. |
| 4 | **Wrong / stale rule data** | An out-of-date income limit produces misleading math. | Income limits are the **official HUD FY2026 MTSP** figures for the metro, effective 2026-05-01, transcribed from the published MassHousing 2026 table. A **Data provenance** panel shows the loaded corpus version, effective date, freeze date, and source; the effective date is shown on every calculation. | Figures were transcribed from the published table for this prototype; a production deploy should load the organizer's frozen pack directly and diff against it. The numbers live in one swappable object. |
| 5 | **Over-confident extraction** | Silently trusting a mis-read field corrupts everything downstream. | Confidence is **computed, not constant**: gross pay is corroborated by a YTD cross-check (`YTD ÷ current gross` must be a clean, date-plausible period count); benefit letters are corroborated by **date-order sanity plus the published 2026 SSI federal-benefit-rate plausibility band**; unreconciled values drop to *needs review*; per-field evidence box; **renter confirmation required**; fields stay **editable after confirmation** and recompute downstream. Field-level accuracy is **measured** by a gold harness (`npm run test:accuracy`), currently 29/29 on the sample corpus. | Image-only (scanned) PDFs are not OCRed — text-layer PDFs and text documents are supported. An LLM extraction path can be added as a second signal (see LICENSE-MANIFEST) without weakening the injection guarantee. |
| 6 | **Privacy leakage / unwanted retention** | Household documents are sensitive. | Client-only, **ephemeral by default** (no server, no training on uploads); **session deletion** wipes memory and any stored snapshot; optional persistence is **AES-GCM encrypted**; a strict **Content-Security-Policy** (header + meta, `default-src 'self'`) blocks every external request, so nothing can exfiltrate even if injected. **Consent is withdrawable:** unchecking the consent box immediately blocks new extraction and offers one-click deletion of already-confirmed documents (tested). The audit log records actions and rule versions **but never raw document contents and never derived financials** (income, household size) — asserted by test. | Encrypted snapshots live in the renter's own browser storage; clearing them is one click. |
| 7 | **Fabricated rule answers** | Invented citations mislead a vulnerable user. | Rules Q&A is **retrieval-only** over an 11-rule corpus with a citation (now including worked examples for the Average Income Test, the 60%-column derivation, and maximum gross rent); every rule cites an external authority — the freshness window cites **HUD Handbook 4350.3 ¶ 5-13.B** (120-day verification term), not an internal placeholder. Scoring requires a minimum token-overlap, and unmatched questions **abstain** ("I won't guess"). | Coverage is limited to the frozen corpus's intents — abstention is the safe default outside them. |
| 8 | **Silent suppression of options** | Hiding choices is a form of gatekeeping. | No ranking/filtering of the renter; the (unbuilt) Discover stretch is specified to show the unfiltered set and never predict acceptance. | Discover is intentionally not implemented in this submission. |
| 9 | **Inaccessible journey** | An inaccessible tool excludes the people it aims to help. | WCAG 2.2 AA practices throughout: single `h1` + correct heading hierarchy, keyboard-complete, visible focus, labeled inputs with `aria-describedby`-linked help *and* errors plus `aria-invalid`, `role="alert"` errors, `aria-live` status region, `role="log"` on the chat and audit logs, `role="search"` on the Q&A form, `role="progressbar"` on packet readiness, labeled stage buttons, non-color status badges, reduced-motion support, and AA-checked contrast in both themes. Verified programmatically: the jsdom pass asserts the h1, roles, `aria-invalid` behavior, and focus management. | An assistive-technology (screen-reader) spot-check with VoiceOver/NVDA is still recommended before a production release; the automated + manual keyboard review is complete. |

**Bottom line:** the safety properties are enforced by construction (no decision path, no
server, retrieval-only answers), so they hold even under adversarial input — not merely by a
disclaimer.
